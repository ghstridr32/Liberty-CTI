This issue of the Alamo Threat Brief covers the period 16–22 March 2026 and is dominated by a single strategic driver: the ongoing U.S.–Israeli military campaign against Iran (Operation Epic Fury / Operation Lion's Roar), launched 28 February 2026. The geopolitical and cyber consequences of this conflict are now the most consequential threat environment for Texas critical infrastructure since the Colonial Pipeline attack of 2021. This brief provides Liberty CTI's structured analytical assessment across all four strategic sectors, addresses active Priority Information Needs, and delivers a 30/60/90-day outlook calibrated to the Texas threat landscape.

Section II

Strategic Context: Operation Epic Fury and the Cyber Dimension

On 28 February 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated airstrikes against Iranian leadership, IRGC facilities, and nuclear infrastructure — an operation designated Epic Fury by the U.S. and Operation Lion's Roar by Israel. Within hours, Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed. Iran's retaliatory response has been immediate, broad, and multi-domain. The cyber dimension of this conflict is now active and escalating.

On 1 March 2026, Iran's IRGC launched Shahed 136 kamikaze drone strikes against two Amazon Web Services data centers in the UAE (ME-CENTRAL-1) and one in Bahrain (ME-SOUTH-1) — the first known military strikes against a commercial hyperscaler's infrastructure in history. AWS confirmed structural damage, power disruption, fire, and water damage affecting availability zones across the region. These strikes set a dangerous precedent: commercial cloud infrastructure is now a confirmed kinetic military target.

Iran's retaliation has been executed under what Flashpoint analysts describe as a pre-planned Mosaic Defense doctrine — a decentralized, asymmetric approach enabling continuous counter-operations even under degraded leadership conditions. The IRGC's cyber arm, operating with the same institutional resilience, has activated multiple concurrent tracks: state-directed APT operations targeting ICS/OT systems; proxy hacktivist campaigns producing DDoS and defacement; and long-prepared wiper and disruptive tools staged for deployment.

CloudSEK's March 2026 reporting confirms that IRGC-linked groups — specifically CyberAv3ngers, APT33, and APT55 — are actively targeting American industrial control systems. The reporting finds CyberAv3ngers actors logging into ICS devices using default credentials and installing malware designed to establish control over those systems. This is not reconnaissance. This is active pre-positioning for disruption.

"Commercial cloud infrastructure, energy systems, and ICS devices are all now simultaneously in the crosshairs. The Iran conflict has collapsed the boundary between geopolitical crisis and operational threat to Texas industries."

— Liberty CTI Analysis, 22 March 2026

On 18–19 March 2026, the conflict reached a dangerous new inflection: Israeli strikes targeted Iran's Asaluyeh complex at South Pars gas field, and Iran retaliated with missile strikes causing "extensive damage" to Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG terminal. Additional Iranian strikes damaged oil refineries in Kuwait and Saudi Arabia. These are not peripheral events for Texas. Ras Laffan and Permian Basin/Gulf Coast LNG are direct commercial competitors and, more importantly, structural pillars of the same global LNG pricing ecosystem.

Section III

Adversary Analysis — Current Posture Assessment

Iran — IRGC-CY / APT33 / CyberAv3ngers

Iran is the dominant near-term cyber threat to Texas critical infrastructure. The IRGC and its Ministry of Intelligence (MOIS) oversee a mature cyber ecosystem combining state-directed APTs and proxy hacktivist collectives. In the current conflict environment, this ecosystem has been activated across multiple operational tracks simultaneously.

APT33 (Refined Kitten / Holmium) — historically focused on the energy sector, with documented campaigns against aviation, petrochemical, and defense firms — is assessed by CloudSEK to be among the active actors targeting American ICS. APT33 has demonstrated destructive capability through the SHAMOON wiper family and has targeted Saudi Aramco and Gulf energy infrastructure in prior campaigns. Texas LNG exporters, refinery operators, and ERCOT-connected utilities share a target profile nearly identical to prior APT33 victims.

Liberty CTI Assessment — Iran

Liberty CTI assesses with HIGH CONFIDENCE that IRGC-affiliated cyber operations against U.S. critical infrastructure are highly likely to intensify over the next 30 days as the kinetic conflict in the Gulf continues. Texas Gulf Coast energy infrastructure — including LNG export terminals at Sabine Pass, Corpus Christi, and Freeport — is assessed as a CRITICAL priority target based on adversary targeting history, strategic significance, and the current geopolitical driver of disrupting U.S. energy market pricing leverage.

China — Volt Typhoon (Voltzite) / APT41

China's Volt Typhoon — tracked as Voltzite by Dragos — continues its long-term pre-positioning campaign inside U.S. critical infrastructure. Dragos CEO Robert Lee confirmed in February 2026 that Voltzite was "getting inside the control loop" of U.S. utility systems in 2025, with the primary intent of future disruption, not current espionage. Dragos further confirmed Voltzite continued breaching U.S. electric, oil, and gas companies in 2025 by compromising cellular gateways and routers as initial access vectors.

CISA, NSA, and FBI joint advisories assess that PRC state-sponsored actors have maintained access in some victim IT environments for at least five years. The Texas ERCOT grid was targeted by Volt Typhoon and successfully defended in 2024, per E-ISAC reporting. However, the breadth of Voltzite's 2025 campaign — and the confirmed OT control loop access at peer utilities — indicates that a single successful defense does not constitute a secure environment.

Liberty CTI Assessment — China

Liberty CTI assesses with HIGH CONFIDENCE that Volt Typhoon / Voltzite's persistent access campaign against U.S. energy infrastructure almost certainly continues in 2026. Texas energy operators should assume that peer utilities have been compromised even if their own networks have not. The China threat is a long-cycle pre-positioning operation — not an immediate disruption actor — but the Iran conflict may alter Beijing's strategic calculus if it sees an opportunity to probe U.S. crisis-response capacity.

Russia — Sandworm (Electrum) / Kamacite

Russia's GRU-linked Sandworm — tracked as Electrum by Dragos — remains the most destructive APT actor in energy sector history, responsible for the 2016 Ukraine power grid attack (Industroyer) and subsequent wiper campaigns. Dragos attributed the December 2025 cyberattacks against Poland's power grid to Electrum. Kamacite, Electrum's initial access provider, conducted a documented reconnaissance campaign against exposed ICS devices in U.S. water, energy, and manufacturing sectors from March to July 2025.

While Russia is primarily focused on the Ukraine conflict and has not yet activated its full ICS disruptive capability against the U.S. homeland, Kamacite's standing reconnaissance posture means that if Moscow decides to escalate against U.S. infrastructure — whether in response to Iran-related policy decisions or Ukraine aid — the access and targeting knowledge are already in place.

Liberty CTI Assessment — Russia

Liberty CTI assesses with MODERATE CONFIDENCE that Russia likely maintains a standing collection and pre-positioning posture against U.S. energy infrastructure but is unlikely to execute disruptive operations against Texas targets in the next 30 days absent a major escalation in U.S. policy toward Russia. The Iran conflict modestly increases the probability that Russia opportunistically tests U.S. crisis-response capacity over the next 60–90 days.

Section IV

Texas Sector Exposure Assessment

⚡ Energy / ERCOT CRITICAL

The Gulf Coast LNG export corridor — Sabine Pass, Corpus Christi, Freeport — is the most geopolitically exposed sector in Texas in the current environment. Iran's March 18 strikes on Ras Laffan directly affect global LNG pricing dynamics in which Texas producers are central actors.

IRGC-CY actors using default credential attacks on ICS devices represent a direct threat to Texas refinery and pipeline SCADA infrastructure. Any Texas energy firm using Unitronics, Schneider Electric, or Honeywell ICS equipment connected to internet-accessible management interfaces should treat this week as a red-letter warning period.

Volt Typhoon's confirmed OT control-loop access at peer U.S. utilities means ERCOT and connected Texas utilities are operating in a compromised peer environment even if their own networks appear clean.

🏛 Finance / Banking HIGH

Texas financial institutions — USAA, Frost Bank, and major DFW banking concentrations — are not primary Iranian targets in the current campaign, but are exposed through two secondary vectors: cloud infrastructure disruption (AWS outages from the Gulf strikes have demonstrated cascading effects on financial services) and opportunistic hacktivist DDoS campaigns running in parallel to state operations.

DPRK Lazarus Group remains the most persistent financial sector threat, conducting cryptocurrency theft operations that have historically targeted U.S. financial firms. The Iran conflict does not significantly alter the DPRK threat posture, which is driven by sanctions-evasion revenue requirements independent of the current crisis.

Ransomware groups — particularly those with Russian-language ecosystem ties — are likely to opportunistically target Texas financial and healthcare organizations while security attention is concentrated on the Iran/energy narrative.

♥ Healthcare / TMC HIGH

The Texas Medical Center — largest medical complex in the world — and the broader Texas healthcare ecosystem remain at HIGH risk from criminal ransomware operators, with RHYSIDA, ALPHV successor groups, and Scattered Spider the primary actors. Healthcare is not a primary Iranian state target in the current cycle, but ransomware groups tracking the news cycle understand that security operations centers are distracted.

Kimsuky (DPRK) data-theft operations targeting U.S. healthcare research and pharmaceutical organizations continue at a baseline level. Texas A&M Health Sciences and UT Health System should maintain heightened monitoring of spear-phishing targeting research-affiliated email accounts.

🛡 Defense Industrial Base HIGH

San Antonio's defense contractor ecosystem — Port San Antonio, JBSA supply chain, and Tier 2/3 contractors supporting 16th Air Force and NSA Texas — remains a CRITICAL focus for Chinese cyber espionage. Volt Typhoon and APT41 targeting of defense-adjacent networks intensifies during U.S. military operations, as China seeks to understand U.S. operational planning, logistics, and weapons system performance data.

The current Iran conflict is providing China with a strategic intelligence collection opportunity: U.S. military and industrial communications about Operation Epic Fury, weapons systems performance, ISR data, and AI-assisted targeting (notably, Claude/Anthropic's AI was reportedly used in the Iran campaign) represent high-value collection targets for PRC MSS-affiliated actors.

Section V

Priority Information Need Responses

PIN-1 GEOPOLITICAL TRIGGERS FOR ADVERSARY CYBER RETALIATION AGAINST U.S. INFRASTRUCTURE

Response: The trigger threshold has been crossed. Operation Epic Fury represents the highest-severity geopolitical trigger event since Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine. Iran's IRGC has operationally activated its cyber retaliation capability. Liberty CTI previously assessed that new OFAC sanctions or a military strike against IRGC leadership would trigger retaliatory cyber operations within 30–45 days. The timeline has compressed: operations began within 72 hours of the February 28 strikes.

The key analytical question is no longer whether Iranian cyber retaliation will occur against U.S. infrastructure, but how destructive that retaliation will become. The IRGC's demonstrated willingness to execute kinetic strikes against commercial cloud infrastructure (AWS data centers, UAE/Bahrain) indicates a threat actor operating with high risk tolerance and escalation authority. Liberty CTI assesses that IRGC-CY operations against U.S. ICS/OT systems — currently at the credential-access and malware-staging phase — likely will progress toward disruptive execution within 30–60 days absent a ceasefire or significant operational setback to Iranian cyber forces. MODERATE CONFIDENCE

PIN-2 CHINESE CYBER ADJUSTMENTS AMID U.S. TECH COMPETITION AND TAIWAN STRAIT TENSIONS

Response: The Iran conflict provides China with an intelligence windfall and a strategic observation window. Beijing is likely using the current crisis to assess U.S. military operational tempo, logistics, AI/cloud dependency in combat operations, and allied resilience — all directly applicable to China's Taiwan Strait contingency planning.

Volt Typhoon / Voltzite maintains its persistent pre-positioning posture. There is no current evidence of China activating disruptive ICS capabilities against U.S. infrastructure in response to the Iran conflict. However, the February 2026 Dragos report confirmation that Voltzite was embedded inside U.S. utility control loops through 2025 means the capability is positioned. If U.S.-China tensions escalate — particularly over Taiwan Strait transits or semiconductor export controls — the probability of activation increases from unlikely to roughly even chance over the subsequent 12 months. MODERATE CONFIDENCE

PIN-3 RUSSIAN DISRUPTIVE OPS IN RESPONSE TO SANCTIONS / BATTLEFIELD DEVELOPMENTS

Response: Russia's Sandworm/Electrum remains postured for escalation but has not activated disruptive operations against U.S. infrastructure in the current cycle. Kamacite's 2025 reconnaissance campaign against U.S. ICS devices confirms a standing collection posture. Russia likely calculates that activating against U.S. infrastructure while the U.S. is engaged in the Iran conflict risks full-spectrum escalation at a moment when U.S. attention is divided — a double-edged strategic calculation.

Liberty CTI assesses that Russia is unlikely to conduct a major disruptive cyber operation against Texas energy infrastructure in the next 30 days but likely will continue collection and pre-positioning activities. The risk calculus shifts if U.S. policy decisions on Ukraine aid or Russia sanctions escalate significantly during the Iran conflict period. MODERATE CONFIDENCE

PIN-4 IRANIAN CYBER ACTIVITY FOLLOWING REGIONAL MILITARY ESCALATION

Response: CONFIRMED ACTIVE. IRGC-affiliated actors are conducting multi-track cyber operations. Track 1: CyberAv3ngers targeting ICS devices with default credentials and custom ladder logic files — consistent with 2023 TTP profile but now deployed at wartime operational scale. Track 2: APT33 and APT55 conducting espionage and disruptive staging operations against American critical infrastructure networks. Track 3: Proxy hacktivist networks generating high-volume DDoS and defacement campaigns to signal political grievance and distract security operations. Over 150 hacktivist incidents were recorded in the first week of the conflict alone (CloudSEK). HIGH CONFIDENCE

PIN-5 TEXAS SECTOR EXPOSURE TO GEOPOLITICAL CYBER PRESSURE

Response: Texas faces a convergence of threat vectors in this cycle. Energy sector exposure is at CRITICAL levels driven by the Iran conflict and the simultaneous persistence of Volt Typhoon's pre-positioning campaign. Financial sector exposure is elevated by the cloud infrastructure disruption precedent set by the AWS data center strikes — Texas financial firms relying on single-cloud architecture should re-evaluate their resilience assumptions. Defense industrial base exposure is elevated by China's opportunistic intelligence collection during U.S. military operations. Healthcare remains primarily at risk from criminal ransomware operators who predictably exploit crisis-driven security operations center distraction.

The common thread across all sectors: Texas organizations that have not conducted tabletop exercises incorporating nation-state ICS attack scenarios in the past 12 months are operating on an outdated threat model. HIGH CONFIDENCE

Indicators to Watch — Collection Requirements

Liberty CTI is monitoring the following indicators as the most consequential signals for escalation or de-escalation of the Texas threat environment over the next 30 days:

Escalation Indicators — Iran

I&W-01: CISA emergency directive referencing energy sector ICS vulnerabilities tied to IRGC-affiliated tooling — would signal active compromise campaign is underway, not just reconnaissance.

I&W-02: New reporting of wiper malware family (consistent with SHAMOON, ZeroCleare lineage) identified in U.S. energy sector networks — would indicate shift from pre-positioning to destructive execution.

I&W-03: IRGC official statements or Iranian state media explicitly naming U.S. energy infrastructure (including specific Texas locations) as retaliation targets — highly likely to precede operational activation.

I&W-04: Anomalous access attempts to Texas refinery, LNG terminal, or ERCOT-connected utility engineering workstations — consistent with Kamacite and IRGC initial access TTPs.

Escalation Indicators — China

I&W-05: New Dragos or CISA reporting on Voltzite / Volt Typhoon activity in ERCOT-connected utility environments — would confirm reconstitution of access after 2024 ERCOT defense success.

I&W-06: CFIUS review of or foreign investment activity targeting Texas semiconductor, defense electronics, or energy technology firms — consistent with PRC economic espionage targeting under cover of commercial relationships.

De-Escalation Indicators

D&E-01: Ceasefire or diplomatic framework for the Iran–U.S.–Israel conflict — would likely reduce immediate IRGC-CY operational tempo within 2–4 weeks but would not eliminate pre-positioned access.

D&E-02: Iran leadership consolidation under a more pragmatic successor to Khamenei — could modestly reduce reckless escalation risk over the 60–90 day horizon.
