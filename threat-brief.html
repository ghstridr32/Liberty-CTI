The Alamo Threat Brief

Weekly cyber threat intelligence for Texas industry — fast executive consumption with source-backed assessments.

Week of: 16 March 2026
Coverage: Energy · Finance · Healthcare · Defense · Gov · Tech
Edition: Vol. 1 · No. 8
Elevated — Iran-linked retaliation activity ongoing across all sectors
This Week's Threats — 10–16 March 2026 06 ITEMS
01/06
Energy

Qilin ransomware targets Texas electric cooperatives; ERCOT grid probing persists

The Qilin ransomware gang — with confirmed Russian ties — claimed attacks on San Bernard and Karnes Electric cooperatives, leaking financial records and board-director PII. The incident follows sustained probing of Texas LNG export infrastructure.

Assessment: Qilin will almost certainly leverage exfiltrated director PII for spearphishing against adjacent co-ops and upstream LNG operators. The gang's pattern — publish, then pivot — suggests a follow-on intrusion attempt against a larger Texas grid asset within 30–60 days, likely using stolen credentials as the entry vector.

SRC Cybernews — Texas Electric Coop Ransomware
02/06
Finance

NY DFS warns financial sector of heightened Iranian cyber threats amid U.S.-Iran conflict

NY DFS issued an emergency advisory on 3 March tied to ongoing U.S.-Israel operations against Iran. Iran-aligned Hydro Kitten has signaled intent to target U.S. financial infrastructure. AI-driven phishing against finance teams is accelerating.

Assessment: Hydro Kitten will likely shift from reconnaissance to disruptive DDoS against U.S. financial platforms as Iran seeks visible retaliation. Texas-based payment processors and regional banks are probable secondary targets — large enough to generate political signal, small enough to lack enterprise-grade mitigation.

SRC NY DFS — Cybersecurity Advisory 3 Mar 2026
03/06
Healthcare

Iran-linked Handala claims Stryker cyberattack; Senate advances healthcare cyber bill

Iran-affiliated hackers disrupted Stryker's global Microsoft environment — the first major U.S. healthcare supply-chain strike since U.S.-Iran hostilities escalated. Senate advanced bipartisan legislation requiring MFA, encryption, and HHS incident-response frameworks.

Assessment: Handala will likely expand beyond Stryker to other U.S. medical device and pharma manufacturers to sustain political pressure. The group prefers targets with high public visibility and downstream hospital dependencies — expect additional supply-chain disruptions timed to U.S. military operational tempo.

SRC Chief Healthcare Executive — Stryker Attack
04/06
Defense

Salt Typhoon telecom espionage continues; Texas orders review of Chinese-made medical devices

China's Salt Typhoon APT has compromised 200+ firms across 80 countries, focusing on wiretap systems and defense-adjacent communications. Texas ordered a cybersecurity review of state agencies' Chinese-manufactured medical devices after federal warnings.

Assessment: Salt Typhoon will almost certainly use persistent telecom access to map the communications patterns of Texas-based defense contractors and cleared personnel — building a targeting package for future collection. MSS will likely move to deactivate or obfuscate implants before formal remediation orders are issued.

SRC Industrial Cyber — TX Device Review & Salt Typhoon
05/06
Government

FBI flags impersonation campaign targeting permit applicants; Texas municipalities remain exposed

FBI warned of a U.S.-wide phishing campaign impersonating local officials to steal permit payments via publicly available zoning data. Texas cities including McKinney, Coppell, and Richardson disclosed attacks over the past year.

Assessment: Impersonation operators will almost certainly expand to utility payment portals and tax collection systems — higher-value transactions with the same OSINT-rich property records as a data source. Ransomware actors monitoring the Mission, TX incident will likely probe other border-region municipalities, anticipating faster payment under crisis conditions.

SRC Digital Forensics Magazine — 13 Mar 2026 Roundup
06/06
Technology

AI-generated "Slopoly" malware enables Interlock ransomware dwell; White House demands active defense

AI-generated malware "Slopoly" gave Interlock ransomware operators undetected access for over a week before exfiltration. The White House's 6-pillar National Cybersecurity Strategy (6 March) formally calls on the private sector to actively hunt and disrupt adversary networks.

Assessment: Interlock will use the Slopoly dwell-time advantage to replicate this playbook against additional managed service providers — AI-generated malware lowers tooling cost while extending pre-exfiltration access windows. As the White House strategy increases threat-hunting pressure, Interlock will likely accelerate intrusion-to-extortion timelines to outpace mandated detection capabilities.

SRC Digital Forensics Magazine — Slopoly / Interlock
