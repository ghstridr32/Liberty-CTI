THE ALAMO THREAT BRIEF
Week of 13 April 2026  |  Liberty Cyber Threat Intelligence  |  San Antonio, Texas

Iran's active disruption campaign against U.S. industrial control systems has entered an acute phase — confirmed by a rare joint FBI/CISA/NSA/CYBERCOM advisory issued April 7. Simultaneous with that escalation, China's Typhoon cluster continues quiet pre-positioning across U.S. energy and telecommunications infrastructure, North Korea's Lazarus Group posted its most efficient financial quarter in history, and ransomware groups are accelerating supply-chain and identity-first attacks against Texas healthcare and DIB targets. This issue examines each threat vector with Texas-specific implications and a 30/60/90-day forward assessment.

⚠ Active Threat
[CRITICAL · PIR-3] FBI/CISA/NSA/CYBERCOM joint advisory (7 Apr): Iranian APT actively disrupting Rockwell Automation PLCs across U.S. energy, water, and government sectors since March 2026.
[ELEVATED · PIR-1] Volt Typhoon LOTL implants assessed to remain in U.S. critical infrastructure. Salt Typhoon telecom espionage ongoing per DHS June 2025 report; Senate inquiry unresolved.
[ELEVATED · PIR-4] DPRK Lazarus Group drained $285M from Drift Protocol DeFi platform (1 Apr 2026) — Q1 2026 total: $309M. Texas financial sector exposure assessed MODERATE.
[HIGH · PIR-2] Ransomware targeting U.S. healthcare and critical manufacturing at record pace; 53 active groups claimed U.S. victims Jan–Feb 2026. Supply-chain compromise vectors surging.

Key Judgments
KJ-1
High Confidence · Almost Certainly
Iranian-affiliated APT actors will continue to escalate operational technology attacks against U.S. energy and water systems throughout Q2 2026, driven by the active U.S.-Iran conflict and a demonstrated willingness to accept disruptive effects. Texas's concentration of Gulf Coast petrochemical facilities, water treatment infrastructure, and Allen-Bradley/Rockwell-dependent OT environments places it in the highest-risk tier for this campaign. The April 7 five-agency advisory confirms the threat has already caused operational disruption and financial loss at victim organizations.
KJ-2
High Confidence · Very Likely
China's Volt Typhoon maintains pre-positioned access in U.S. critical infrastructure and will not activate disruptive capabilities absent a Taiwan-contingency trigger or major geopolitical escalation. However, the presence of dormant implants means any Texas energy or telecom operator that has not actively hunted for living-off-the-land (LOTL) artifacts should assume a non-negligible probability of silent compromise. Salt Typhoon telecom espionage continues to affect communications metadata with implications for executive and sensitive business communications.
KJ-3
High Confidence · Almost Certainly
DPRK's Lazarus Group will sustain financially motivated cyber operations targeting cryptocurrency, DeFi platforms, and financial services infrastructure through 2026, generating hard currency to fund Pyongyang's weapons programs outside sanctions reach. The $285M Drift Protocol heist on April 1 — executed in under 11 seconds — signals a qualitative capability leap in speed of action. Texas financial institutions with crypto exposure, DeFi integrations, or fintech partnerships face elevated third-party risk.
KJ-4
Moderate Confidence · Likely
Ransomware groups will increasingly pivot to supply-chain and identity-first compromise vectors against Texas healthcare and DIB targets in Q2–Q3 2026, reducing the value of perimeter-based defenses and exploiting third-party vendor access as the primary initial attack vector. The industrialization of attack automation — compressing time-to-exploit for disclosed PoCs — makes unpatched systems a near-certain liability within days of vulnerability disclosure, not weeks.
KJ-5
Moderate Confidence · Likely
The Trump administration's proposed $491M cut to CISA — eliminating 1,083 positions and reducing the agency to 2,649 staff — will materially degrade federal early warning, coordination, and incident response capacity available to Texas private-sector operators, increasing the decision-making burden on individual organizations and the value of commercial intelligence providers as a force multiplier. This gap is structural and will not be resolved in the near term.

Sector Threat Posture — Week of 13 April 2026
Energy / ERCOT
THREAT LEVEL: SEVERE

ERCOT Grid & Gulf Coast Petrochemical

The April 7 CISA advisory directly implicates energy-sector OT as an Iranian targeting priority. Texas operates the only major grid island in the continental U.S., making it uniquely vulnerable to cascading effects from SCADA/HMI disruption. Gulf Coast refinery and LNG export facilities rely heavily on Allen-Bradley PLCs — the exact device class currently under active Iranian exploitation. Volt Typhoon LOTL pre-positioning in transmission infrastructure remains an unresolved background risk.

Primary Actors: IRGC-CY (Iran), Volt Typhoon (PRC)  |  PIR-1, PIR-3
Healthcare / TMC Ecosystem
THREAT LEVEL: SEVERE

Texas Medical Center & Regional Systems

Healthcare led all sectors in ransomware volume in January 2026 (27 incidents). Texas AG Paxton's active demands for answers from BCBS Texas and Conduent following a data breach signal intensifying regulatory scrutiny. The Iranian Handala group's remote-wipe attack on Stryker (March 2026) demonstrates Iran's expanding willingness to disrupt U.S. medical infrastructure — a new capability threshold. AI-enabled attacks compressing access-to-impact timelines pose acute risk to clinical operations dependent on uptime.

Primary Actors: Ransomware ecosystems, Handala (Iran)  |  PIR-2, PIR-3
Financial Services

Texas Banks, Fintech & Crypto-Adjacent

Threat Actor Assessments
PIR-3 · Iran
IRGC-CY / Handala / CyberAv3ngers
Updated: 13 Apr 2026
WHAT
Iranian APT actors are actively exploiting internet-exposed Rockwell Automation/Allen-Bradley PLCs across U.S. energy, water, and government facilities. Attacks involve malicious manipulation of project files and HMI/SCADA display data — causing operational disruption and financial loss confirmed at multiple victim sites since March 2026.
WHO
IRGC-affiliated APT group; Handala responsible for Stryker remote-wipe (March 2026) using Microsoft Intune as a weaponized MDM tool; CyberAv3ngers previously attributed to 2023–2024 Unitronics PLC campaign. The current campaign is assessed to be a coordinated IRGC-directed effort tied to the ongoing U.S.-Iran conflict that began in February 2026.
WHY
Retaliation for U.S.-Israel strikes against Iran beginning February 2026. Tehran cannot match U.S. conventional military capability; cyber disruption of private-sector infrastructure is the asymmetric lever available to impose costs and signal resolve without crossing thresholds that invite kinetic retaliation.
WHAT NEXT
Liberty CTI assesses with HIGH CONFIDENCE that Iranian OT targeting will expand in scope and geographic breadth over the next 30–60 days. Texas Gulf Coast water and energy OT environments using internet-connected Rockwell/Allen-Bradley devices are at elevated risk. Expect escalation to ICS manipulation beyond display-data — including potential attempts to alter control logic or force emergency shutdowns.
PIR-1 · China
Volt Typhoon / Salt Typhoon / Flax Typhoon
Updated: 13 Apr 2026
WHAT
Volt Typhoon (PLA) maintains persistent, pre-positioned implants in U.S. critical infrastructure using LOTL techniques — blending into legitimate network traffic without malware signatures. Salt Typhoon (MSS) has compromised telecommunications infrastructure in 200+ organizations across 80 countries, including accessing lawful intercept (CALEA) systems. Congressional inquiry to AT&T and Verizon CEOs remains unresolved as of April 2026.
WHO
Four coordinated PRC APT groups: Volt Typhoon (disruption/pre-positioning, PLA-linked), Salt Typhoon (espionage, MSS-linked), Flax Typhoon (IoT botnet infrastructure), Brass Typhoon (supply chain). Each serves a distinct role within China's doctrine of "active defense" — enabling both intelligence collection and crisis-contingency disruption options.
WHY
Preparing for a Taiwan contingency that requires degrading U.S. military logistics, public confidence, and inter-sector coordination. The objective is deterrence through demonstrated capability, not immediate disruption. PRC cyber strategy has shifted from economic espionage to politically driven operations with pre-positioned strike options.
WHAT NEXT
Liberty CTI assesses with HIGH CONFIDENCE that Volt Typhoon will not activate pre-positioned access in the near term absent a Taiwan crisis trigger. However, Salt Typhoon espionage activity — particularly against telecom infrastructure routing Texas executive and government communications — will continue with no near-term containment. Texas businesses reliant on compromised telecom carriers should treat communications metadata as observable by PRC intelligence.
PIR-2 · Russia
Russian Ransomware Ecosystem — Qilin, Akira, Clop, Play, DragonForce
Updated: 13 Apr 2026
WHAT
53 ransomware groups claimed U.S. victims in January–February 2026, with an estimated 750–800 total U.S. organizations hit in that window. Healthcare led with 460 incidents; financial services 258. Top active strains: Qilin, Akira, Clop, INC, Play, DragonForce. Supply-chain attacks on SaaS integration providers (April 7 reports) created lateral access to multiple client organizations in a single compromise event.
WHO
Primarily Russia-based ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) ecosystems operating with implicit Kremlin tolerance. State-sponsored GRU/SVR units maintain separate but parallel interest in U.S. energy and financial infrastructure disruption — particularly relevant as Ukraine conflict sustains geopolitical pressure on U.S. posture.
WHY
Financial motivation (ransom payments declining but still profitable; criminal groups chasing larger, fewer targets). Geopolitical alignment provides safe harbor for operators. Healthcare and critical infrastructure remain prime targets because operational disruption creates maximum payment incentive within patient-safety risk constraints.
WHAT NEXT
Supply-chain attack vectors targeting shared service providers — SaaS platforms, MSPs, healthcare IT vendors — will accelerate. AI-enabled automation is compressing time from vulnerability disclosure to active exploitation. Texas healthcare systems sharing vendors across the TMC ecosystem face amplified cascade risk if a single shared provider is compromised.

Liberty CTI assesses with HIGH CONFIDENCE that DPRK will continue large-scale crypto and DeFi platform targeting through 2026. Texas fintech firms, digital asset custodians, and financial institutions with DeFi or crypto integrations face elevated third-party risk. DPRK IT worker infiltration remains an active threat to software development firms — particularly those holding cleared personnel data or financial system code.
Evidence-Based Analysis · Supporting the Key Judgments
Intelligence Vignettes
7 April 2026 · FBI / CISA / NSA / EPA / DOE / CYBERCOM

Iran Disrupts U.S. Energy and Water PLCs — Active Since March 2026

A five-agency joint advisory confirmed that Iranian-affiliated APT actors have been exploiting internet-exposed Rockwell Automation/Allen-Bradley PLCs across U.S. critical infrastructure since at least March 2026. Attacks include malicious manipulation of project files (.ACD format) and HMI/SCADA display data. Multiple victims experienced operational disruption and financial loss. The campaign escalated following the onset of U.S.-Israel military strikes against Iran in February 2026.

The advisory identifies MITRE ATT&CK TTPs: Initial Access (TA0001), Command and Control (TA0011), and Impact (TA0040) — confirming intent to cause operational effects, not merely reconnaissance.

So What for Texas Texas Gulf Coast petrochemical, water treatment, and power generation facilities rely heavily on Allen-Bradley OT systems. Any internet-connected Rockwell PLC in a Texas critical infrastructure environment should be treated as an active targeting priority for IRGC-affiliated actors. Disconnecting OT devices from the internet is the single highest-value mitigation available today.
1 April 2026 · Drift Protocol / DPRK Attribution

Lazarus Group Drains $285M from Solana DeFi Exchange in Under 11 Seconds

At 16:06:09 UTC on April 1, 2026, North Korean-linked actors drained the major vaults of Drift Protocol — the largest decentralized perpetual futures exchange on Solana — of approximately $285M in user assets. The attack was completed before any human could intervene. Attribution rests on blockchain analytics consistent with prior Lazarus laundering patterns and the characteristic speed of DPRK post-compromise laundering operations.

This attack follows the $1.5B Bybit heist (February 2025) and establishes that DPRK has industrialized its capability to execute nine-figure thefts with near-zero dwell time between access and execution.

So What for Texas Texas financial institutions with any exposure to DeFi protocols, crypto custody, or fintech partnerships supporting digital assets should treat their third-party risk assessments as materially incomplete without explicit DPRK supply-chain threat modeling. The attack vector is social engineering at the developer/infrastructure layer — not smart contract exploits.
March 2026 · Handala / Iran

Iran-Linked Handala Group Deploys Remote Wipe Against Stryker Corporation

Iranian hacking group Handala conducted a disruptive cyberattack against Stryker — a major U.S. medical technology company — by weaponizing Microsoft Intune, the company's own mobile device management platform. Hackers manipulated the MDM system to remotely wipe thousands of employee devices, causing brief disruption to Stryker's manufacturing, ordering, and shipping capabilities. The FBI has attributed Handala to leaking FBI Director Kash Patel's private email account contents in the same operational period.

So What for Texas The Stryker attack establishes a critical precedent: Iran is now willing to use legitimate IT management tooling as a destructive weapon against U.S. healthcare and manufacturing companies. Texas Medical Center member institutions using cloud-based MDM or endpoint management platforms should audit administrative access controls and geographic anomaly detection immediately.
2025–2026 · Texas (Multiple Jurisdictions)

Ransomware Wave Hits Texas Municipalities — Twenty-Plus Local Governments Affected

Texas DIR led response to a coordinated ransomware attack affecting at least 20 Texas local government entities in what became a declared state-level emergency. In the preceding months, Mission, Matagorda County, McKinney, Coppell, and Richardson all reported separate ransomware incidents impacting government services — including hospitals, utilities, and emergency services. Recovery operations across affected jurisdictions required deployment of Texas Military Department, Texas A&M CSOCC, and DIR teams.

So What for Texas The shared IT vendor and inter-dependency structure between Texas municipalities, healthcare systems, and utilities creates a cascade vulnerability: a single shared-services provider compromise can trigger simultaneous multi-sector disruption. Private-sector operators in energy and healthcare co-located with municipal systems (water, power billing) face secondary exposure.
December 2025 · Salt Typhoon / PRC

Salt Typhoon Compromises U.S. House of Representatives Committees

Intrusions detected in several U.S. House of Representatives committees in December 2025 were attributed to Salt Typhoon, extending the PRC espionage campaign from telecommunications infrastructure to legislative branch communications. The DHS June 2025 report confirmed Salt Typhoon access to a state Army National Guard network. Per FBI, Salt Typhoon has now hacked at least 200 companies across 80 countries.

So What for Texas Any Texas executive whose communications transit compromised major U.S. telecom carriers should assume that call records, metadata, and potentially audio are accessible to PRC intelligence services. Sensitive business negotiations, M&A discussions, and regulatory engagement involving Texas energy, finance, or DIB companies should be conducted exclusively over end-to-end encrypted platforms.

The Iranian campaign's demonstrated willingness to cause OT disruption combined with ransomware groups' simultaneous healthcare targeting creates a non-trivial scenario risk that was not present 18 months ago.

Decision Advantage · Observable Signals
Indicators & Warnings
◈
OT/SCADA anomalies on Allen-Bradley PLCs: Unexpected changes to PLC project files (.ACD), HMI display data inconsistencies, or controllers shifting to remote-run mode without authorization. Treat as active Iranian exploitation until proven otherwise — not as maintenance artifact.
◈
Unusual outbound SMB or WMI activity in IT environments: Volt Typhoon's LOTL signature. Monitor for use of native Windows admin tools (netsh, wmic, PowerShell) generating low-volume, off-hours traffic to external IPs. Standard EDR misses this; requires behavioral anomaly detection.
◈
Cisco router configuration changes at network edge: Salt Typhoon primary vector. Unexplained changes to Cisco edge device configurations, new admin accounts, or unusual SSH sessions from foreign-geolocated IPs are strong indicators of ongoing PRC telecom espionage access.
◈
Unexpected MDM bulk-action commands or device enrollment anomalies: Mirrors the Stryker attack vector. Admin-issued remote wipe commands, unusual geographic enrollment of new devices, or MFA bypass attempts in Microsoft Intune or similar MDM platforms indicate potential IRGC pre-positioning.
◈
Developer or IT staff engagement with unsolicited crypto or DeFi recruitment: Primary DPRK TraderTraitor access vector. Any unsolicited high-value job offers, DeFi advisory engagements, or token grant offers targeted at engineers or infrastructure staff should be treated as potential DPRK social engineering.
◈
Shared vendor incident notifications involving healthcare SaaS or billing platforms: Supply-chain ransomware trigger. A single breach of a shared Texas healthcare IT vendor can simultaneously compromise multiple TMC-ecosystem institutions. Vendor incident notifications should trigger immediate defensive posture elevation, not just passive monitoring.
◈
Escalation in U.S.-Iran conflict intensity or new rounds of U.S. strikes: Geopolitical trigger for intensified Iranian cyber operations within 72 hours. If kinetic conflict escalates, Texas energy and water OT operators should assume near-term Iranian cyber activity is probable, not possible.

Decision Advantage · Executive and Operational Actions
Recommended Actions
Board / Executive — Immediate (0–72 hours)

Iran OT Emergency Response

  • Direct OT/SCADA team to immediately audit all internet-connected Allen-Bradley/Rockwell Automation PLCs and disconnect those with no operational requirement for internet access.
  • Require report within 48 hours on which OT systems have network segmentation between IT and OT environments — and which do not.
  • Brief board on April 7 joint advisory; frame as an active campaign, not a theoretical risk.
Executive — Near-Term (7–30 days)

Communication Security Uplift

  • Mandate end-to-end encrypted communication (Signal, Wickr) for all executive, M&A, regulatory, and sensitive business discussions given confirmed Salt Typhoon telecom compromise.
  • Brief board and C-suite on assumption that call records and metadata on major U.S. telecom carriers may be observable by PRC intelligence.
  • Review external counsel and banking relationship communication channels.
Security Operations — Immediate

Volt Typhoon / LOTL Hunt

  • Initiate behavioral anomaly hunt for LOTL indicators: native Windows admin tool misuse, off-hours WMI/netsh activity, credential dump artifacts in ESE Application Logs.
  • Extend log retention beyond default Windows policy — Volt Typhoon exploits short log retention to erase evidence.
  • Audit Cisco edge device configurations for unauthorized changes or anomalous admin accounts.
Security Operations — Near-Term

Identity & Third-Party Controls

  • Audit MDM platform (Microsoft Intune, Jamf) admin access — validate MFA, geographic anomaly detection, and bulk-action approval workflows given Stryker attack vector.
  • Map all shared vendors, SaaS providers, and IT service providers; establish incident notification SLAs and breach escalation playbooks.
  • Implement phishing-resistant MFA (hardware keys or passkeys) for all privileged accounts and external-facing access.
Financial / Fintech — Strategic

DPRK Financial Threat Posture

  • Assess and disclose (to board) any operational dependency on DeFi protocols, digital asset platforms, or fintech providers with crypto exposure.
  • Establish vendor due diligence requirements for crypto custody and DeFi integration partners that explicitly address DPRK threat modeling.
  • Review IT and developer hiring practices — validate identity verification against DPRK IT worker infiltration risk.
Strategic — All Sectors (30–90 days)

Intelligence Gap Mitigation

  • Given CISA capacity reduction, build or procure commercial threat intelligence capability to replace federal early-warning that is being structurally degraded.
  • Establish direct relationships with Texas Cyber Command (TXCC at UTSA), Texas DIR, and InfraGard San Antonio for public-private coordination on ransomware and OT threats.
  • Develop and exercise a sector-specific incident response playbook for Iranian OT disruption scenario within 90 days.
