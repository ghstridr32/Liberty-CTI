The Domain

Cyber Is the Only Domain Where
Every Organization Is Already Forward Deployed — Whether Leadership Recognizes It or Not

Adversaries do not distinguish between public and private targets — or between your organization and your competitors. They map your organization as terrain — and they have already done it.

Access Point

A pathway into a higher-value target through your networks, suppliers, or shared infrastructure.

Leverage Point

An organization whose disruption creates maximum operational and financial pressure at minimum cost to the adversary.

Intelligence Target

A source of commercial, technical, or strategic information that informs adversary decision-making.

Disruption Node

A system whose failure cascades across sectors — creating second-order business disruption and amplifying one operation into a national event.

Sector Intelligence

How the Threat Impacts Your Sector

Five industries. Five active threat campaigns. One shared environment — and no organization is isolated from it.

⚡Energy

Pre-Positioned for Coercion

Volt Typhoon — PRC state-sponsored — has achieved persistent access inside U.S. critical infrastructure to enable future disruption, not exfiltration. ERCOT's isolated grid cannot compensate from adjacent systems. The Permian Basin and Gulf Coast are among the most strategically targeted energy assets in the country.

"If your business touches energy, you are already operating inside a target environment built for coercion — not just intrusion." → Energy & ERCOT Intelligence
💰Financial Services

Identity as the Attack Surface

Scattered Spider and related clusters have made social engineering the dominant financial attack vector — defeating architectures built to stop technical exploits. Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, and Austin form one of the densest financial target concentrations in the country.

"The most damaging financial cyber event breaks trust before it breaks systems — and that loss is immediate and measurable." → Financial Services Intelligence
🏥Healthcare

Supply Chains as Leverage

Iran-linked Handala targeted Stryker's medical device supply chain in March 2026 as a geopolitical coercion instrument — not opportunistic crime. The Texas Medical Center serves 10M+ patients annually. A comparable disruption here carries consequences at national scale.

"Your organization does not need to be in a war zone to become part of one — disruption is already reaching commercial environments." → Healthcare Intelligence
🛡️Defense & Aerospace

The DIB as an Intelligence Target

Nation-state actors exploit the weakest link in defense supply chains to reach the strongest. San Antonio's ecosystem — JBSA, NSA Texas, and a dense contractor base — creates a high-density target environment where smaller organizations are frequently the access point adversaries use to reach prime contractors.

"Being adjacent to national security is not protection — it increases your likelihood of being targeted. In the adversary's calculus, it is an invitation." → Defense Industrial Base Intelligence
🖥️Technology & Data Centers

Contested AI and Cloud Terrain

Texas is becoming a national AI and cloud infrastructure hub. A compromise of a major hyperscale operator creates simultaneous downstream exposure across every sector. The attack surface for any Texas organization now includes infrastructure it does not own and cannot directly protect.

"If you rely on the cloud, you are already on contested terrain — and your risk extends beyond what your team can directly control — and the attack surface includes your power company."
Key Judgment

"What happens in Texas does not stay in Texas — disruption here drives national consequences. Disruption here is a national event."

Texas controls roughly a quarter of U.S. domestic energy production, hosts the world's largest medical complex, and is home to a growing share of U.S. AI and cloud infrastructure. Adversaries have mapped this concentration. Most Texas organizations have not.
