Texas Is Operating in a Contested Cyber Domain, and Many Organizations Are Not Postured for It

Major Texas industries operate inside a cyber domain shaped by nation-state actors, criminal networks, and proxy organizations. This is not only a future risk. It is the current environment, and many organizations remain underprepared despite existing security investments.

The Domain

Cyber Is a Domain Where
Organizations Can Become Operational Terrain

Adversaries do not always distinguish between public and private targets, or between your organization and your competitors. They map organizations as terrain for access, leverage, and disruption.

Access Point

A pathway into a higher-value target through your networks, suppliers, or shared infrastructure.

Leverage Point

An organization whose disruption creates maximum operational and financial pressure at minimum cost to the adversary.

Intelligence Target

A source of commercial, technical, or strategic information that informs adversary decision-making.

Disruption Node

A system whose failure can cascade across sectors, creating second-order business disruption and amplifying one operation into a wider event.

"If it is connected, it can become terrain. Cyber risk now reaches organizations whose leadership may not see themselves as part of the operating environment."

Sector Intelligence

How the Threat Impacts Your Sector

Five industries. Distinct threat pressures. One shared environment, and no organization is fully isolated from it.

⚡Energy

Pre-positioning Risk

PRC-linked actors, including Volt Typhoon, have been publicly associated with pre-positioning in U.S. infrastructure for potential future disruption. ERCOT's isolated grid, the Permian Basin, and the Gulf Coast remain priority risk environments.

"If your business depends on energy, you are operating inside an environment exposed to coercion risk, not just intrusion risk." → Energy & ERCOT Intelligence
💰Financial Services

Identity as the Attack Surface

Scattered Spider and related clusters show how social engineering can defeat architectures built to stop technical exploits. Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, and Austin form a dense concentration of financial targets.

"A damaging financial cyber event can break trust before it breaks systems, and that loss is immediate and measurable." → Financial Services Intelligence
🏥Healthcare

Supply Chains as Leverage

Iran-linked Handala claimed activity involving Stryker's medical device supply chain in March 2026. The Texas Medical Center serves more than 10 million patients annually, making comparable supplier disruption a national concern.

"Organizations do not need to be in a conflict zone to experience spillover risk from geopolitical disruption." → Healthcare Intelligence
🛡️Defense & Aerospace

The DIB as an Intelligence Target

Nation-state actors often use weaker points in defense supply chains to reach higher-value programs. San Antonio's ecosystem, including JBSA, NSA Texas, and a dense contractor base, creates a high-density target environment where smaller organizations can become access pathways.

"Being adjacent to national security does not remove risk. It can make an organization a more attractive collection target." → Defense Industrial Base Intelligence
🖥️Technology & Data Centers

Contested AI and Cloud Terrain

Texas is becoming a national AI and cloud infrastructure hub. A compromise of a major hyperscale operator creates simultaneous downstream exposure across every sector. The attack surface for any Texas organization now includes infrastructure it does not own and cannot directly protect.

"If you rely on the cloud, your risk extends beyond what your team directly controls, including the power and connectivity dependencies that keep compute available."
Key Judgment

"Disruption in Texas can produce national consequences because the state sits across energy, defense, healthcare, finance, and compute dependencies."

Texas controls roughly a quarter of U.S. domestic energy production, hosts the world's largest medical complex, and is home to a growing share of U.S. AI and cloud infrastructure. Adversaries have mapped this concentration. Most Texas organizations have not.
