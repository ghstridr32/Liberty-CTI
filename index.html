Our Mission

Turning Warning Into
Executive Action.

Texas is one of the most target-rich operating environments in the country. Its energy infrastructure, financial networks, defense industrial base, healthcare systems, and digital growth corridors are all exposed to state-backed actors, criminal organizations, and cascading business disruption.

Our mission is to help leadership teams see risk early, align faster, and act before disruption spreads. We bring the discipline of national-security decision support to commercial clients who need clear business relevance, not technical noise or government jargon.

01
Anticipate
Identify risk before it turns into downtime, financial loss, or a board-level crisis.
02
Attribute
Understand who is driving the threat, why your business is exposed now, and how serious the risk really is.
03
Advise
Translate warning into clear decisions for senior leaders, boards, and operators who cannot afford to guess.

We are not a cybersecurity vendor. We do not sell software, manage firewalls, or respond to incidents. We help leadership teams understand what could go wrong, where the organization is exposed, and what actions matter before disruption forces their hand.

Founding Principle
Liberty CTI was founded by two U.S. Air Force officers who spent their careers supporting national security decision-makers at the highest levels of government. Liberty CTI extends that experience into the private sector for leaders who need sharper warning, tighter coordination, faster decisions, and fewer surprises.

Our Approach

Built for Decisions.
Not Dashboards.

Most firms tell you what is happening. Liberty CTI helps you understand what it means, what it could affect next, and what leadership should decide before disruption compounds. That is the difference between information and decision support.

02

03

Why It Matters

What Happens When
Leadership Lacks Strategic Warning.

Most organizations do not fail because they lack security tools. They fail because leadership teams are forced to make high-consequence decisions too late, with incomplete context, and without a clear view of how one disruption could spread across the business.

01
Leaders React Instead of Prepare
The organization is pushed into crisis mode before executives understand what matters, what is exposed, or what must happen next.
02
Teams Operate on Conflicting Assumptions
Security, operations, legal, finance, and communications move at different speeds without a shared picture of the problem.
03
Business Impact Multiplies
What could have been managed as a contained risk becomes a broader disruption affecting revenue, customers, regulators, and reputation.
Our Vision

Helping Texas Leaders
See Across the Next
Disruption.

The threat landscape facing Texas's most critical industries is shaped by more than opportunistic cybercrime. It is shaped by geopolitical competition, criminal ecosystems, supply-chain concentration, and cross-sector dependencies that can turn one incident into a much broader business disruption.

Organizations must understand these dynamics not as background context, but as decision support that informs investment, governance, communications, and operational risk posture. The question is no longer whether your sector is a target. The question is whether leadership sees the risk early enough to act.

Strategic warning is not a luxury. In the current environment, it is a requirement for protecting operations, preserving revenue, and avoiding executive surprise.
