Our Mission

Closing the
Intelligence Gap.

Texas is the eighth-largest economy in the world. Its energy infrastructure, financial networks, defense industrial base, and healthcare systems are not abstract targets — they are active priorities for foreign intelligence services and criminal organizations operating at scale.

Our mission is to close the gap between what government agencies understand about these threats and what industry leaders are positioned to act on. We apply the same analytical tradecraft used at the highest levels of the U.S. Intelligence Community — adapted for commercial clients who need clarity, not clearances.

Founding Principle
Liberty CTI was founded by two U.S. Air Force officers who spent their careers supporting national security decision-makers at the highest levels of government. Liberty CTI extends that experience — and that standard — into the private sector.

Our Approach

Intelligence First.
Always.

Most cybersecurity firms are built around technology. Their value proposition is software, detection, and response. That work is necessary — but it is not intelligence. Liberty CTI is built around analysis.

01
Geopolitical Context

Cyber threats do not emerge from a vacuum. We ground every assessment in the geopolitical environment — understanding how state competition, sanctions pressure, and military posture shape adversary targeting decisions before they manifest in the digital domain.

02
Adversary Intent

We assess not just capabilities, but objectives. Understanding what an adversary is trying to achieve — and why they are targeting your sector now — is the intelligence that transforms reactive security into proactive organizational resilience.

03
Calibrated Confidence

Our work is aligned with accepted Intelligence Community analytical practices. Every assessment carries calibrated confidence language, structured analytic tradecraft, and transparent sourcing. Our clients receive intelligence products, not marketing reports.

Our Vision

Protecting the
Economic Engine
of Texas.

The threat landscape facing Texas's most critical industries is no longer shaped solely by opportunistic criminal actors. It is shaped by geopolitical competition — by foreign governments that view energy infrastructure, financial systems, and defense supply chains as legitimate targets in a contest that is already underway.

Organizations must understand these dynamics — not as background context, but as operational intelligence that informs investment, governance, and risk posture decisions at the board level. The question is no longer whether your sector is a target. The question is whether you understand who is targeting you and what they intend to achieve.

Strategic awareness is not a luxury. In the current environment, it is a requirement for organizational resilience.
