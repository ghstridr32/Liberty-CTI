Who We Are

Not pedigree.
Proven instinct.

In military vernacular, a mustang is a former enlisted service member who becomes an officer. The title means more than career progression. It signals leaders shaped by real experience, grounded perspective, and the kind of survival instinct that is hard to fake and harder to break.

That identity sits at the center of Liberty CTI. We did not build this firm from résumé polish or consulting templates. We built it from years spent in high-pressure environments where ambiguity was constant, time was limited, and leaders still had to make the call.

Why We Built This

We saw the gap.
We built the bridge.

Between us, we spent three decades watching intelligence get delivered to senior leaders in formats that demanded translation. Analysts described threat activity with precision. Briefings were technically accurate. And yet the people in the room — the ones who had to actually decide — were left to determine what it meant for them.

That gap is not a content problem. It is a framing problem. Cyber threat intelligence has been built for security teams, not for boards. It answers "what happened?" when the people who matter most are asking "what does this mean for my organization, and what do I do before it gets worse?"

When we left government, we knew two things. First, the threats targeting Texas energy infrastructure, financial systems, healthcare networks, and defense suppliers are not theoretical — they are active, documented, and accelerating. Second, nobody was providing decision-grade intelligence to the executives responsible for those sectors in a format they could actually use.

Liberty CTI is the answer to both. Not another vendor. Not another dashboard. An intelligence firm built by people who spent careers producing analysis for the hardest decisions in the world — and who know exactly what it takes to make intelligence move action.

How We Think

Judgment,
not just information.

Most cyber threat intelligence tells you what happened. That matters, but it is not enough for a senior decision-maker. Leaders need to know what matters, why it matters now, what changes next, and what demands action before a crisis forces the decision.

That is the Liberty CTI difference. Our work is shaped by national security environments where intelligence had to drive decision advantage, not just fill a page. We integrate threat activity with policy context, strategic implications, and operating environment so leaders can act with confidence.
