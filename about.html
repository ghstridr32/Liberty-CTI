Your Story

From the Bronx
to the Situation Room.

Luis Maldonado did not begin his career in a boardroom or a think tank. He started in July 1997 as a Russian Linguist in the enlisted ranks of the U.S. Air Force — learning to listen, to analyze, and to produce intelligence under conditions that demanded precision and left no margin for error.

That origin shapes everything about how Liberty CTI approaches its mission. This is not an organization built on theoretical frameworks or vendor certifications. It is built on three decades of doing the actual work — collecting intelligence, fusing it across domains, leading hundreds of analysts, and commanding the units responsible for identifying the very cyber threats now targeting organizations like yours.

The Bronx taught him that perspective matters more than pedigree. The military taught him that intelligence without action is just information. The White House Situation Room taught him how senior leaders make decisions under uncertainty — and what kind of analysis is genuinely useful when it matters most.

Liberty CTI exists because that perspective belongs in the private sector. Texas's most critical industries face the same adversaries that government agencies track daily — but receive none of the same warning. We close that gap.

Why Liberty CTI Exists

The Intelligence
Gap Is Real.

After three decades inside the U.S. Intelligence Community, the pattern was impossible to ignore: the organizations most exposed to nation-state cyber threats are often the least equipped to understand them. Not because they lack resources. Because they lack access to the kind of intelligence that was never designed for them.

Government agencies produce extraordinary intelligence. But they produce it for government consumers. The result is a structural gap — Texas energy companies, financial institutions, defense contractors, and healthcare systems face the same adversaries that government agencies track daily, but receive none of the same strategic warning.

Liberty CTI was built to close that gap. Not by offering another security product or compliance framework — by producing actual strategic intelligence that helps senior leaders understand not just what happened, but what it means and what comes next before a crisis forces their hand.

// The Problem
Executives are overwhelmed with data — threat feeds, vendor alerts, compliance reports, incident summaries. None of it answers the question that actually matters: what should we do, and why right now?
// The Gap
Technical cybersecurity firms detect events. Managed security providers monitor networks. Incident responders clean up breaches. None of them produce strategic intelligence on adversary intent before the event occurs.
// The Consequence
Organizations that lack decision-ready intelligence make reactive decisions — spending resources against last year's threats while the adversary has already moved on to the next campaign.
// The Liberty CTI Answer
"Executives are overwhelmed with data but lack decision-ready intelligence. We produce the intelligence — not the data."

Co-Founder & Principal
Luis Maldonado
Lieutenant Colonel, USAF (Ret.) · Russian Linguist · Cyber Intelligence Officer

A native of the Bronx, New York, Luis Maldonado started his Air Force career as an enlisted Russian Linguist in 1997 and spent three decades ascending through every meaningful level of U.S. intelligence work. Commissioned in 2009 as a distinguished graduate of Officer Training School, he went on to serve in ISR, electronic warfare, cyber, and special operations roles across the globe — including Afghanistan, Qatar, and assignments across the U.S. national security enterprise.

His career culminated at the intersection of intelligence and executive decision-making — as a Duty Officer in the White House Situation Room, then as Director for Legislative Affairs at the National Security Council. As commander of the 35th Intelligence Squadron, he led a 270-person cyber intelligence unit with a $6 million operational budget and developed the analytical framework at Liberty CTI's core.
